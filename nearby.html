Guest questions

Frequently asked questions

What can I do near RYST 109A?

Guests can walk to the private beach, visit Paramankeni backwaters, go boating at Raindrop Boat House, explore Alamparai Fort, photograph Marakkanam salt pans, and take day trips to Mahabalipuram's UNESCO monuments, Pondicherry's French quarter, Auroville and Covelong for surfing.

How far is Mahabalipuram from RYST 109A?

Mahabalipuram is approximately 38 km from RYST 109A — about a 45-minute drive north along the ECR, making it an easy day trip for temples, stone monuments, cafes and beach time.

How far is Pondicherry from RYST 109A?

White Town and the Pondicherry promenade are approximately 63 km south — usually around a one-hour drive, depending on traffic. Auroville is a further 12 km beyond Pondicherry.

Is there boating or water activity nearby?

Yes. Raindrop Boat House and the Paramankeni backwater area are around 2 km from RYST 109A, making boating one of the easiest nearby outings. Surfing lessons are available at Covelong Beach, about 62 km north.

Which attractions are best for families with children?

The private beach, backwater boating, Krishna's Butter Ball, the India Seashell Museum, Mahabalipuram monuments and Paradise Beach are especially suitable for families. Plan around heat — early morning or late afternoon visits work best.

Can I cover Mahabalipuram and Pondicherry in one stay?

Yes, easily. RYST 109A sits centrally between both destinations so guests can plan one day for Mahabalipuram and another for Pondicherry and Auroville — two distinct experiences without changing your base.
